DevOps Summary

by Ian McLuskie

This is a Summary of the first 5 pages of the Document that describes DevOps, a set of project management techniques by IBM Cloud Thought Leadership White Paper.

        DevOps started as a set of practices involving the streamlining the software delivery process that over time have been recognized and utilized by start-ups and large enterprises alike. It utilizes a lot of cloud-based technology to help correlate the differing goals of various groups such as IT and programmers. It’s a process that reduces the lag between changes in the market and customer demand, making the changes necessary for product development faster with more consistent releases, as well as promoting innovation. Continuous customer feedback is essential to this project. As well as automation and goal alignment throughout a project.
        IBM seeks to achieve these things by, firstly, making a work environment that promotes many of these wanted practices. People are encouraged to “fail fast” penalty-free, while organized in smaller self-organizing squads; all supported by metrics that increase transparency and customer input. Within this overarching culture is a process for development. Think, Code, Deliver, Run, Manage, Learn, and then repeat. This creates a feedback loop, when organized within the corporate culture that ensures the best and most innovative ideas are implemented, refined, and built upon.
